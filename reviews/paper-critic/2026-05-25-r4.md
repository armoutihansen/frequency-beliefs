### 2. Lemma statement vs proof
- The lemma asserts \(\sup_{p\in P_M(r)}\sum_i a_i p_i = \sup_{c\in[0,1]} \sup\{\sum_i a_i p_i : p \in \Sigma(c)\}\), then specialises to \(a=(\1\{i\le j\})\) for cumulative-sum bounds, then to quantile-index bounds via the displayed min/max identities.
- The proof (`08_appendix.tex:323–340`) establishes (i) \(P_M(r)=\bigcup_c \Sigma(c)\) by Proposition `prop:manhattan`; (ii) sup-over-union = sup-of-sups; (iii) the cumulative-sum specialisation by direct substitution; (iv) the quantile-index identities from monotonicity of \(S_j(p)\) in \(j\). Each lemma claim is matched line-for-line. No gap.
- The descriptive sentence in §3.4 ("the identified mean is a linear functional of \(p\) optimized over the same threshold-indexed box-simplex slice", line 278) is consistent with the lemma's general statement applied at \(a=x\).

## Minor Issues

### m1 — Variance row promised but missing from table
- **Category:** Tables & Figures
- **Location:** `paper/sections/03_scoring_rules.tex:336`
- **Quote:** `"Variance bounds follow from a quadratic program over the same identified sets and are reported in the supplementary verification output."`
- **Problem:** The §3.6 paragraph (line 293) and the abstract sentence (line 41) foreground variance alongside median/quantile, and the table caption references variance — but `tab:functional-menu` has no Variance row. A reader scanning the table for the variance bound (a natural first move after reading the caption) finds nothing and is redirected to "supplementary verification output", which is not a label in the paper.
- **Fix:** Either (a) add a `Variance \(\mathrm{Var}(Y)\)` row to the tabular with the QP-computed bounds, or (b) rephrase the caption sentence to "Variance bounds are computable on the same identified sets via a quadratic program; we omit them from the table for compactness and report them in `scripts/consistency_check.py`." Option (b) is the 1-line fix.

### m2 — Hedge wording invites a "but the table contradicts it" reaction
- **Category:** Academic Tone
- **Location:** `paper/sections/03_scoring_rules.tex:340`
- **Quote:** `"with the ranking on the median possibly differing from the ranking on the mean."`
- **Problem:** At the displayed report \(r=(2,2,2,2,2)\), the rankings on mean and median *agree* — Discrete tightest on both. The "possibly differing" claim is honest as a general statement but slightly awkward when the worked example doesn't exhibit it, because the reader's eye goes from the prose ("ranking possibly differs") to the table ("ranking agrees here") and registers a small mismatch.
- **Fix:** Replace with: `"with the ranking on the median not in general identical to the ranking on the mean (Table~\ref{tab:functional-menu} happens to display a report where the rankings agree; reports off the symmetric centre separate them)."` This is honest, anticipates the reader's check, and preserves the no-universal-winner framing.
