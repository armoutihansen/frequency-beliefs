## Major Issues

### M1 — Single-equation lemma proof misstates monotonicity of φ

**Category:** Math exposition (new content from round 2)
**Location:** `paper/sections/08_appendix.tex:320` (inside proof of `lem:manhattan-unimodal`)

**Quote (verbatim):**
> "Both arguments of the minimum are continuous and weakly decreasing in \(c\), so \(\phi\) is weakly decreasing."

**Problem:**
The second argument of `min` is `1 − Σ_{v≠i} p_v^{(L)}(c)`. Because `p_v^{(L)}(c)` is weakly decreasing in `c`, `Σ p_v^{(L)}(c)` is weakly decreasing, and therefore `1 − Σ p_v^{(L)}(c)` is weakly *increasing* — not decreasing. The function `φ(c) = min(p_i^{(U)}(c), 1 − Σ p_v^{(L)}(c))` is the minimum of a decreasing function and an increasing function, which is **unimodal** (rises then falls, peaking at the crossing). The proof's subsequent case analysis exploits exactly this unimodality and reaches the correct conclusion, but the declarative sentence quoted above is wrong as stated and contradicts the very next paragraph of the proof. A careful referee will flag the inconsistency.

This is the only substantive blemish in an otherwise tight new lemma+proof. The unimodality framing in the body (`03_scoring_rules.tex:275` — "a function that is unimodal in c") is correct; the appendix should match.

**Fix:**
Replace the offending sentence with:
> "The first argument is weakly decreasing in \(c\), the second weakly increasing, so \(\phi\) is unimodal, with maximum at the crossing point \(c^*\)."

The rest of the case analysis works unchanged. No mathematical content is altered — only the descriptive monotonicity claim is corrected to match the actual structure of `φ`.

### M2 — Oxford-UK spelling regression in new §3.4 paragraph

**Category:** Consistency / LaTeX-Specific (round 1's spelling normalisation partly undone by new content)
**Location:** `paper/sections/03_scoring_rules.tex:277, 282`

**Quotes (verbatim):**
> "The sharp \emph{mean} bounds traverse the same threshold parameterisation: the identified mean is a linear functional of \(p\) optimised over the same threshold-indexed box-simplex slice, computed on a fine grid of \(c\)-values (with reported tolerance) rather than by a one-dimensional root-find." (line 277)

> "...the mean bounds are sharp on the same threshold parameterisation but are implemented as a grid optimisation over the threshold." (line 282)

**Problem:**
Round 1 standardised the paper on Oxford UK conventions: `-ize/-ization` (so `minimize`, `maximize`, `normalize`, `optimize`, `parameterize`, `characterization`, `maximization`, `minimization` throughout) combined with `-our/-re/-ce` (`favour`, `centre`, `centrepiece`, `behaviour`, `behavioural`). The new §3.4 paragraph — added in this round to address round 1's BL3 — uses `parameterisation`, `optimised`, `optimisation` in `-ise/-isa-` form, which is the *British (non-Oxford)* convention and inconsistent with the rest of the paper. (`parameterization` and `optimization` already appear elsewhere with `-iz-`.) A regression introduced by the round 1 fix itself.

**Fix:**
- Line 277: `parameterisation` → `parameterization`; `optimised` → `optimized`
- Line 282: `parameterisation` → `parameterization`; `optimisation` → `optimization`

(Two-line global replace; no semantic effect.)

## Minor Issues

### m1 — Cross-reference in abstract

**Category:** Style
**Location:** `paper/main.tex:41`

**Quote:** "...two further count-loss rules, Hamming and Chebyshev distance, violate it, and Section~\ref{sec:other-rules} takes them up as the boundary of the approach."

**Problem:** Abstracts are often consumed detached from the body (indexing services, search engines, conference programmes). A `\ref` to an in-body section may render as a number with no context, or simply break. Most journals discourage cross-references in abstracts.

**Fix:** Replace `Section~\ref{sec:other-rules}` with descriptive language, e.g., "the discussion takes them up..." or "a closing section discusses them...". Keep the body `\ref` calls.

### m2 — Missing comma before non-restrictive "which" in abstract

**Category:** Grammar
**Location:** `paper/main.tex:42`

**Quote:** "...with a closed-form crossover in the number of positive-report coordinates which a design comparison then locates as a crossover in the Dirichlet concentration parameter."

**Problem:** The "which" clause is non-restrictive (it elaborates rather than narrows). A comma is required before it.

**Fix:** Insert comma: "...positive-report coordinates, which a design comparison then locates as a crossover in the Dirichlet concentration parameter."

### m3 — Lemma label name does not match lemma title

**Category:** Labelling
**Location:** `paper/sections/08_appendix.tex:299`

**Quote:** `\begin{lemma}[Single-equation characterization of Manhattan coordinate bounds]\label{lem:manhattan-unimodal}`

**Problem:** The label slug `manhattan-unimodal` advertises a unimodality result, but the lemma's actual headline is "single-equation characterization". Unimodality of φ is a step inside the proof, not the lemma's statement. Cosmetic; no semantic effect.

**Fix (optional):** Rename to `lem:manhattan-bound` or `lem:manhattan-single-eq`, and update the two `\ref` calls in `03_scoring_rules.tex:275-276`. Or accept the harmless misalignment — the label is internal to the LaTeX source.
