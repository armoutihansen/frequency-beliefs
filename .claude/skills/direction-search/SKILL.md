# Direction Search Skill

Use this skill when asked whether the project is taking the right direction, whether there are better framings, or how to turn the current draft into the strongest possible paper.

## Goal

Explore alternative directions without introducing speculative or unsupported claims into the paper.

## Procedure

1. Read the current project state.
2. Identify the current implicit paper.
3. Generate candidate directions.
4. For each direction, assess:
   
   **Scientific validity:**
   - research question;
   - contribution;
   - fit with current results;
   - risk of overclaiming;
   - risk of being too incremental;
   - risk of being too broad.
   
   **Practical feasibility:**
   - proof burden;
   - simulation burden;
   - feasibility;
   - literature dependence.
   
   **Publication readiness:**
   - audience;
   - publication fit.

5. Rank directions.

6. Recommend one direction and explain why.

7. Identify what must be true for the recommendation to hold.

8. Identify what evidence would change the recommendation.

## Candidate direction types

Consider:

- methodological note;
- mechanism-design paper;
- partial-identification paper;
- scoring-rule theory paper;
- experimental-design paper;
- behavioral-economics elicitation paper;
- computational design-comparison paper;
- appendix-heavy technical note;
- split into two papers.

## Output format

Use:

1. Current implicit paper
2. Candidate directions
3. Ranking
4. Recommended direction
5. Required changes
6. Required literature
7. Required proofs
8. Required simulations
9. Main risks
10. Next three tasks

## Rules

- Creativity is allowed.
- Speculation must be labeled.
- Do not propose manuscript edits unless the direction is viable.
- Do not recommend expanding the paper merely because a connection is interesting.
- Prefer a sharp publishable paper over a broad unfocused one.
